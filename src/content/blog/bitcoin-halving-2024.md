Every ~210,000 blocks — roughly every four years — Bitcoin's block subsidy is cut in half. This programmatic supply reduction, known as the halving, is one of the most anticipated recurring events in crypto markets.

## The Supply Shock Mechanism

Bitcoin miners receive newly minted BTC as a reward for securing the network. Before the 2024 halving, this reward was 6.25 BTC per block. Post-halving: 3.125 BTC. This represents approximately 450 new BTC per day removed from the daily issuance rate.

The thesis: if demand holds constant while new supply entering the market drops by half, upward price pressure follows.

## What Past Halvings Show

The 2012 halving (subsidy from 50 to 25 BTC) preceded a roughly 8,000% price increase over the following year. The 2016 halving (25 to 12.5 BTC) preceded approximately a 2,800% increase. The 2020 halving (12.5 to 6.25 BTC) was followed by an approximately 700% increase.

Returns are diminishing — unsurprising as Bitcoin's market cap grows — but the directional pattern has been consistent.

## The Institutional Variable

The 2024 cycle introduced a materially new factor absent from previous halvings: the approval of spot Bitcoin ETFs in the United States in January 2024. BlackRock, Fidelity, and others began offering regulated, institutional-grade Bitcoin exposure for the first time.

This created a sustained demand-side dynamic that historically had no parallel. The interaction between programmatic supply reduction and unprecedented institutional demand makes the 2024 cycle analytically distinct from its predecessors.

## On-Chain Indicators

Key metrics to watch around halving events include:
- **HODL Waves**: the distribution of UTXO age bands, indicating whether long-term holders are distributing
- **Exchange Reserves**: declining exchange BTC balances signal reduced sell-side pressure
- **Miner Net Position Change**: miners accumulating vs. selling post-halving
- **Realized Price**: the aggregate cost basis of all BTC, useful for identifying structural support

These metrics don't predict markets. They describe the current state of supply and demand in a framework that removes short-term noise.
